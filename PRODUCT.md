# Product

## Register

brand

Note: this repo has two surfaces. `agency-site/` (this project's primary surface) is **brand** — a marketing site where design IS the product. `agency-dashboard/` is a separate **product**-register internal tool (Next.js app) and is out of scope for this design pass.

## Users

Owners of small landscaping and lawn care companies in Northeast Ohio (Twinsburg, Hudson, Solon, Macedonia, Aurora). They're busy running crews and bidding jobs, not thinking about websites. Many have an outdated, DIY, or nonexistent website and found this page either via a cold outreach email/call or a free mockup offer. They're skeptical of agencies/SaaS pitches and respond to plain-spoken, locally-relevant, trustworthy presentation — not hype.

## Product Purpose

Buckeye Web Co.'s marketing site exists to convince a local landscaping business owner that this is a small, capable, locally-relevant operator who will build them a site that gets them more calls — and then handle everything ongoing so they never have to think about it again. Success = booked "free mockup" calls from real NE Ohio landscaping companies.

## Brand Personality

Premium & polished — refined typography, generous whitespace, and restrained motion that signal "we do quality work," while staying grounded and approachable (not corporate-SaaS cold). Confident without hype. Plain-spoken copy, no jargon.

## Anti-references

Avoid the generic AI/SaaS-startup look: no cream/beige/parchment body backgrounds, no gradient text, no tiny uppercase tracked "eyebrow" labels above every section, no identical 3-card grids repeated section after section, no side-stripe colored borders on callouts, no stock hero-metric templates. The site should feel custom-built for a landscaping business, not a generic template with the word "landscaping" swapped in.

## Design Principles

1. **Premium through restraint** — quality reads through typography, spacing, and craft details, not through saturation or decoration.
2. **Locally grounded, not corporate** — visuals and copy should feel like a real small operator who understands landscaping/lawn care, not a faceless SaaS company.
3. **Vary section rhythm** — no two consecutive sections should use the same layout pattern (grid of 3 cards, etc.); each section earns its own structure.
4. **Show outcomes, not features** — emphasize what the business owner gets (more calls, less hassle) over technical feature lists.
5. **One clear CTA path** — "Get my free mockup" / "Book a free call" should be the obvious next step throughout, without competing CTAs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, visible focus states on all interactive elements (already added), `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation, semantic heading hierarchy, keyboard-operable mobile nav.
